<p><p>The chromatography systems market encompasses various techniques for separating and analyzing chemical components. Gas chromatography (GC) is used for volatile substances, employing gas as the mobile phase. Liquid chromatography (LC) involves liquid solvents for analyzing non-volatile compounds. LC-MS (liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry) combines LC with mass spectrometry for enhanced identification and quantification. Similarly, GC-MS (gas chromatography-mass spectrometry) pairs GC with mass spectrometry for detailed analysis of gaseous samples. Each method serves diverse applications across pharmaceuticals, environmental testing, and food safety.</p></p>
