<p><p>The spinal trauma device market is categorized into internal and external devices. Internal devices involve surgical implants such as rods, screws, and plates that stabilize the spine from within, often used for correcting spinal deformities or fractures. External devices, on the other hand, encompass braces, casts, and traction systems that provide external support and immobilization to facilitate healing. Both types aim to enhance recovery and restore mobility in individuals suffering from spinal injuries or related conditions.</p></p>
