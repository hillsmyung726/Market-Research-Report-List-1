<p><p>Breast lesion localization devices are crucial for guiding surgeons during breast cancer surgery. Wire localization involves placing a thin wire into the breast to pinpoint the lesion. Radioisotope localization uses radioactive materials to identify the lesion via imaging techniques. Magnetic tracers utilize magnets to help locate tumors non-invasively. Other methods may include ultrasound-guided localization or 3D imaging technologies. Each type enhances surgical accuracy and improves patient outcomes by ensuring precise removal of breast lesions.</p></p>
