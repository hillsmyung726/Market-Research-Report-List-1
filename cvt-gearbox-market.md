<p><p>The CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission) gearbox market includes two primary types: Belt-Chain Drive CVT and Planetary CVT. Belt-Chain Drive CVT uses a flexible belt or chain to transfer power between pulleys, offering smooth acceleration and fuel efficiency. Planetary CVT employs a system of gears and planetary sets to achieve seamless gear ratios, providing high torque capacity and durability. Both types cater to automotive applications, with Belt-Chain CVT being more common in passenger vehicles and Planetary CVT in heavy-duty or performance-oriented vehicles.</p></p>
