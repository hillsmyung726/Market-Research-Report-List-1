<p><p>Industrial refrigeration coolers are categorized into several market types based on the refrigerants used. HFCs (hydrofluorocarbons) and HFOs (hydrofluoroolefins) are common due to their effectiveness in cooling with lower environmental impact. NH3 (ammonia) is favored in larger systems due to its high efficiency and low cost. CO2 (carbon dioxide) is increasingly used for its eco-friendliness, while glycol serves as a secondary refrigerant, particularly in chillers, to enhance cooling efficiency. Other types include natural refrigerants and synthetic blends, focusing on sustainability and performance.</p></p>
