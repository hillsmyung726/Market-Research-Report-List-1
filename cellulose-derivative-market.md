<p><p>The cellulose derivative market encompasses three primary types: cellulose ethers, cellulose esters, and a combination of both. Cellulose ethers are water-soluble compounds used as thickening agents and stabilizers in various industries, including pharmaceuticals and food. Cellulose esters, on the other hand, are largely used as film-forming agents and in coatings, plastics, and textiles due to their excellent mechanical properties. The combined market leverages the unique features of both derivatives, resulting in diverse applications across multiple sectors.</p></p>
