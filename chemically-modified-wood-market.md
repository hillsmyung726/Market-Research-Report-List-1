<p><p>The Chemically Modified Wood market includes types like Acetylated Wood and Furfurylized Wood. Acetylated Wood is treated with acetic anhydride, enhancing durability, stability, and resistance to decay by altering its cell structure. Furfurylized Wood involves impregnating wood with furfuryl alcohol, improving hardness, dimensional stability, and resistance to biological degradation. Both processes create eco-friendly, high-performance materials suitable for outdoor applications, decking, and construction, offering sustainable alternatives to traditional treated wood while reducing environmental impact. These modifications extend wood lifespan and reduce maintenance needs.</p></p>
