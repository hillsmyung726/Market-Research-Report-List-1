<p><p>An Energy Recovery Ventilator (ERV) is a system designed to improve indoor air quality while maintaining energy efficiency. It works by exchanging stale indoor air with fresh outdoor air, transferring heat and moisture between the two streams to reduce energy loss. This process helps regulate temperature and humidity levels, making ERVs ideal for residential, commercial, and industrial applications.</p><p>The Energy Recovery Ventilator Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing awareness of indoor air quality, rising energy costs, and stringent building regulations promoting energy efficiency. Emerging markets in Asia-Pacific and the Middle East are also contributing to the market's expansion due to rapid urbanization and infrastructure development. Overall, the ERV market is poised for robust growth, driven by technological innovation and increasing emphasis on energy conservation and indoor air quality.</p></p>

<p><p>The Energy Recovery Ventilator (ERV) market includes various types of heat exchangers. Plate and Frame Type Heat Exchangers use flat plates to transfer heat and moisture between air streams, offering compact and efficient designs. Heat Pipe Heat Exchangers utilize sealed pipes with refrigerant to transfer heat, ideal for moderate climates. Rotary Type Heat Exchangers feature rotating wheels to exchange heat and humidity, suitable for large-scale applications. Other market types include innovative designs like thermosiphon or enthalpy cores, catering to specific ventilation needs and energy efficiency goals.</p></p>
