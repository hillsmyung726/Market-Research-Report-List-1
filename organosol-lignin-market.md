<p><p>The organosol lignin market includes various processing methods such as ethanol/water pulping, known as the Alcell process, and pulping with acetic acid, typically referred to as the CIMV process. The Alcell process utilizes a mixture of ethanol and water to extract lignin from biomass, enhancing yield and purity. Conversely, the CIMV process employs acetic acid to achieve effective lignin separation, which can result in high-quality products. Both methods contribute to the sustainable production of lignin for various industrial applications.</p></p>
